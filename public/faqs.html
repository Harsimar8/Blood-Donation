FAQs

Who can donate blood?

Most healthy adults aged 17-65 and weighing over 50 kg can donate. Check specific guidelines if unsure.

How often can I donate?

You can donate whole blood every 8 weeks. Other types of donations may have different intervals.

Is it safe?

Yes, donating blood is safe. Sterile, single-use equipment is always used.

How long does it take?

The whole process takes about an hour, with the actual donation lasting 10 minutes.

What should I do before donating?

Eat well, stay hydrated, and bring an ID. Avoid fatty foods before your donation.

What happens to my blood?

Your blood is tested, processed, and separated into components for medical use.

Can I donate with a tattoo?

Wait a few months after getting a tattoo or piercing before donating.

Do I need to prepare before donating?

Yes, eat well, stay hydrated, and avoid alcohol. Bring an ID and wear comfortable clothing.

Can I donate if I'm on medication?

Some medications may affect your ability to donate. It's best to consult with the donation center beforehand.

How will I feel after donating?

Most people feel fine after donating, but it's good to rest, stay hydrated, and avoid strenuous activities for the rest of the day.

Can I donate if I've traveled recently?

Travel to certain areas may affect your ability to donate. Check with your local donation center if you're unsure.

What types of blood donations are there?

You can donate whole blood, platelets, plasma, or double red cells. Each type has different benefits and requirements.
